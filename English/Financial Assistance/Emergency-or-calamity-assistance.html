EMERGENCY OR CALAMITY ASSISTANCE


The main source of immediate emergency or calamity assistance for Persons with Disabilities (PWDs) in the Philippines is the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) through its programs.

DSWD Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situation (AICS)

    The Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situation (AICS) is the primary program of the DSWD that serves as a social safety net to support the recovery of individuals and families from unexpected crises, including natural or man-made disasters.

    Medical Assistance: Financial aid to cover hospitalization expenses, cost of medicines, and the provision of assistive devices.

    Other Support Services: Financial assistance for other needs like burial, transportation, or educational expenses.

How to Avail of AICS:

    1. Go to the nearest DSWD Crisis Intervention Unit (CIU/CIS/SWAD Office) or to the Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) in Pasig.

    2. Required Documents typically include:
    One (1) valid identification card of the client, which includes a PWD ID.
    Barangay Certificate of Residency or Indigency, or a Certificate that the client is in need of assistance.

    For Calamity Victims: Documents applicable to the crisis, such as a Police Report or a Certificate from the Local Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (LDRRMO).

    3. You will undergo an interview and assessment by a DSWD Social Worker to determine your eligibility and the appropriate type and amount of assistance.

Malasakit Centers

    Malasakit Centers established in various public hospitals can also facilitate access to the DSWD's financial assistance based on the AICS guidelines, primarily focusing on medical and financial needs for indigent and financially incapacitated patients.

    Other Related Programs and Considerations

    1. SSS Calamity Loan
    If you are an active member of the Social Security System (SSS), you may be eligible to apply for the Calamity Loan Assistance Program (CLAP), provided your residence is in an area declared under a state of calamity by the NDRRMC. This is a loan, not a grant.

    2. Assistance from Local Government Units (LGUs)
    Your local government unit (LGU) is generally the first responder. They are mandated to implement programs for PWDs, and their local social welfare office (CSWDO or MSWDO) is key in coordinating and providing immediate relief during a disaster. You should approach the LSWDO or your Barangay Hall for localized calamity assistance.

    3. Disaster Risk Reduction and PWD Inclusion
    There are ongoing efforts to ensure that PWDs are included in disaster preparedness and response. If you are part of a PWD organization, they can often serve as a channel for getting and sharing information and accessing adapted emergency resources.

    Next Step for Assistance
    If you or a PWD you know is in crisis due to an emergency or calamity, the most immediate and effective next step is to contact the nearest Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) in your city or municipality.
